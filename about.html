About Us

Fetal MRI Community [FMRC] Founded – April 2024, New Delhi.

Background: With rapid advancements in imaging technology, Fetal MRI has now emerged as an important modality for imaging of the fetus. Fetal MRI is a key ‘problem-solving’ imaging option after the initial Ultrasound assessment. Fetal MRI offers an additional dimension for the comprehensive evaluation of the fetus in obstetric practice. The incremental value of Fetal MRI in wide range of clinical settings offers additional information for better prognostication of the fetus and allows better parental counselling and evidence-based clinical decision-making.

As per Medical Termination of Pregnancy (Amendment) Act, 2021, the gestational limit for termination of a pregnancy in India has been increased from 20 to 24 weeks, in special cases. This allows an additional window period of 4 weeks when MRI may be optimally performed for detailed assessment of the fetal structures and to rule out the possibility of an underlying structural abnormality. This is likely to have positive impact on Fetal MRI in India in terms of its use in clinical practice.

Fetal MRI Community [FMRC] is an initiative to collectively embark on this journey towards advanced ‘multi-modality fetal imaging’ where ultrasound is offered as the first-line imaging option and fetal MRI as a robust ‘problem-solving’ imaging modality.

Vision and Objectives:

  • 1. To serve as the common platform for the Radiologists and other experts who utilize Fetal MR Imaging in clinical and academic practice.
  • 2. To establish and continually improve the acquisition technique and protocols for fetal MRI studies.
  • 3. To understand and implement the learning points for optimal image interpretation in Fetal MRI.
  • 4. To set highest standards of fetal MRI practice in India with positive impact on patient care.
  • 5. To create greater awareness among clinicians and patients regarding the role of Fetal MRI.
  • 6. To work towards the growth and advancement of the clinical fetal MRI practice in India.
  • 7. To promote the practice of advanced ‘multi-modality fetal imaging’ where ultrasound is offered as the first-line imaging option and fetal MRI as a robust ‘problem-solving’ imaging modality.

President's Address

Distinguished faculty, esteemed colleagues, and dear friends,

It is indeed my privilege and a moment of immense pride to welcome you all to the 4th Fetal MRI Course organized by the Fetal MRI Community and Indraprastha Apollo Hospital New Delhi.

With an objective to promote Fetal MRI, FMRC aim to establish fetal MRI as an integral, accessible, and clinically relevant tool across the length and breadth of Country.

Despite the availability of MRI technology across the country, fetal MRI services are still limited to a relatively small number of centres. This presents both a challenge and tremendous opportunity for the FMRC. It is time to take the initiative and integrate fetal MRI into routine clinical practice at every Radiology department in every medical college and academic institution in India. This vision can only be achieved through collaboration between radiologists and with our clinical colleagues.

On this occasion, it is important to understand that as the field of Fetal MRI is growing, its growth calls for meaningful research, which is not isolated, but collaborative; not theoretical, but clinically impactful. Right from improvising the imaging sequences to improve image quality, to describing newer patterns in prediction of fetal brain cortical malformations to exploring early biomarkers of placental insufficiency, we have a sea of opportunity to contribute to knowledge that can truly enhance science and influence outcomes. Above all, our commitment must remain anchored in better patient care and fetal care. Every image we acquire, every interpretation we make, and every protocol we refine must ultimately serve one purpose—to improve the lives of mothers and their unborn children.

This course is more than an academic gathering. It is a platform to inspire change, to build capability, to upgrade skills and to strengthen the field of Fetal Radiology in India. I encourage each one of you to engage actively, learn deeply, and take this knowledge forward. Because the true success of FMRC will be reflected not in our meetings, but in the impact, we create across centres and across lives.

Our annual Fetal MRI Courses have now become an important platform for training the radiologists and to assist in establish fetal MRI services in their institutions. The 5th course will be organized at the Kovai Medical Center & Hospital, Coimbatore in April 2027. Fetal MRI Community will otherwise also support and help the members in this initiative across the length and breadth of country. We also plan to schedule CMEs and webinars for our referring clinicians as well to create awareness and to update about the strengths of fetal MRI.

I am thankful to all members of Fetal MRI Community including the executive committee members for their valuable support, dedication and enthusiasm in advancing this evolving field.

I also thank our beloved parent organization – Indian Radiology & Imaging Association [IRIA] for all the support to FMRC and we indeed look forward to work closely under the SAMRAKSHAN initiative of IRIA.

I am immensely thankful to our industry partners – Philips Healthcare & Vivere Imaging for their valuable support to our academic programs.

Let us work together to make 2026 a landmark year for fetal MRI in India.

“Let this be the BEGINNING of a National movement.”

Thank You.
